# Priya Nair — pm pack sample persona

> **Fictional.** All handles carry the `-fictional` suffix (`linkedin.com/in/priyanair-fictional`, `priyanair-fictional.substack.com`), per the Jane Doe convention.

## Who she is

Group PM, enterprise, at Miro. Eleven years from support queue to product org. Chicago, then remote. Writes decision memos the way other people write diaries.

## The arc

Industrial engineering at Michigan, then the unglamorous door into product: support ops at Zendesk, where cutting ticket backlog 31% taught her that every queue is a product with no PM. Shopify took her onto checkout — +12% completion across two years of payment-flow experiments, half of which failed and taught her more than the winners. Intercom made her a senior PM on onboarding; +34% new-user activation is the headline, but the work was killing a beloved tour feature the data said nobody finished. Miro since late 2023: enterprise expansion, +19% seat growth in her first full year.

Two public artifacts back the story: **Shipping Notes**, a Substack of decision memos (3,800 subscribers), and a set of **redacted PRD teardowns** on her site — nine real PRDs, redactions cleared with each employer, because hiring managers in this field trust artifacts over adjectives.

## Why the pm pack fits

- **Experience leads, with metric-delta headlines** — every role card opens on the delta (+12%, +34%, +19%), with the decision story in the body. The deltas are relative on purpose: the pack's rule for confidential metrics, and her actual NDA position.
- **Shortlist signals she holds:** case studies front-loading Problem / My Role / Key Outcomes; embedded redacted-with-permission artifacts (the accepted norm per the spec); named trade-offs — the killed tour feature is told as a trade-off, not a victory lap.
- **Bin signals she avoids:** no "improved engagement" vagueness — every claim carries a number and a denominator period; no feature lists without business outcomes; no process essays floating free of artifacts.
- **Explicit role honesty:** each card carries a "My role:" line — the checkout work was a team of nine; she says so.

## What she wants next

Director of product at a company where enterprise and self-serve fight for the same roadmap — she thinks that tension is the job, not the obstacle.
