## The arc

BS in Communication at Ohio State, then the classic ladder with no skipped rungs: SDR at ADP (127% of meeting quota averaged over six quarters), mid-market AE at Zendesk (104–138% across 14 quarters — she quotes the range, not just the peak, because the floor is the credibility), enterprise at Datadog (Club ×3), strategic accounts at Snowflake since 2023 (118% FY25, nine new logos). MEDDICC-trained in 2019; it shows in how she documents wins — economic buyer, paper process, the competitive displacement, all filed.

## Why the sales pack fits

Sales is the pack where the portfolio's job is narrowest: the pre-screen is résumé-only — titles, quota %, progression — and the brag book is deployed live in-interview, at the exact moment performance is questioned. Dana's page leads with a stat-row (quota %, rank, Club years) because numbers-first, minimal-prose is what survives that screen; everything else supports retrievability. The pack's confidentiality line is hers too: customer names and deal sizes stay out (her metrics are attainment percentages and logo counts, never contract values), and earnings evidence — W-2s, comp statements — is in-room material, never published. Manager and client quotes carry the social-proof layer with role-level attribution.
