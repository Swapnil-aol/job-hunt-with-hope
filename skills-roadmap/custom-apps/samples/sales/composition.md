## Demand-side fidelity

- **Shortlist signals honored**: verifiable numbers (stack rankings, quota-attainment records, awards) organized for retrieval in seconds; the brag book itself appears as a project — documentary-proof framing.
- **Bin signals avoided**: no superlatives without evidence (no "top performer" prose — the ranks speak); no unverifiable quota claims (ranges and quarters cited, floor included); no published earnings or deal sizes (in-room material, per the pack rule).

## Extrapolation notes — what Hope generalizes vs what is Dana's

1. **Generalizes**: stat-row as lead app, numbers-first minimal prose, the confidentiality gate (customer names / deal sizes / comp evidence withheld) — every sales persona gets these.
2. **Generalizes**: quote-wall eligibility (trust-economy role) — quotes only from documents the user provides.
3. **Dana-specific**: the floor-included quota range ("104–138%") — Hope should keep a user's honest range when offered, but never synthesize one from a single peak number.
4. **Dana-specific**: senior modulation — multi-year attainment + progression carries the page. A junior rep leads with activity metrics and ramp speed instead.
5. **Dana-specific**: the brag book as a visible project. It generalizes only to users who actually maintain documented win evidence; Hope offers it, never fabricates it.
