# Marisol Vega — educator pack persona

> **Fictional.** Sample persona for the educator role pack ([`../../SPEC-role-packs.md`](../../SPEC-role-packs.md) §3). Handles carry the `-fictional` suffix, same convention as `assets/fixtures/persona-jane-doe/`. Persona id: `marisol`.

## Who she is

Marisol Vega, 35, middle-school science teacher in Mesa, Arizona — thirteen years in, National Board Certified since 2022, now department lead. Her file isn't built on credentials; it's built on what her students did: a 22-point average gain in state science scores over her first five years, and a current campus running 84% proficiency against a 61% district average. She's looking at instructional-coach and curriculum-specialist roles, which is what finally made her build a portfolio.

- LinkedIn: `linkedin.com/in/marisol-vega-fictional`

## The arc

BS in Biology at Arizona State, certified in 2013, straight into a Title I seventh-grade classroom in Phoenix. The MEd (2014–2016, while teaching) reshaped her from content-deliverer to curriculum-builder: her Desert Ecology Field Unit — written from scratch, field-tested in her own classroom — has since been adopted by six schools. Moved to Mesa in 2018 as eighth-grade science and department lead. The Family Science Nights she started in 2021 have drawn 300+ families across twelve events; National Board Certification followed in 2022. Longitudinal growth data is her favorite slide, and she can show it for every cohort she's taught.

## Why the educator pack fits

Principals flip straight to student-impact evidence and classroom artifacts — the demo lesson is the real screen, and the portfolio's job is to earn it. The pack leads with credentials only briefly (Education/Certifications first, because licensure is a binary gate), then moves to where Marisol is strongest: self-created lesson artifacts and growth data, all anonymized. The FERPA hard gate is the defining constraint and she's a clean case for it: every student work sample is stripped of identifying marks, growth data is aggregate-only, and there are no classroom photos in the portfolio at all — consent files don't exist, so the gallery-grid stays off. Block, don't ask twice. Quotes from students and parents are paraphrased with consent notes or left out.
