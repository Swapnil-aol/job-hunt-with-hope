## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

The principal flips to student-impact evidence and classroom artifacts; the demo lesson is the real screen. So:

1. **Hero**: name · "Middle-school science · NBCT · dept lead" · 13 yrs
2. **Education/Certifications** (default-open): license + NBCT, scannable in seconds
3. **Experience** with growth data in the card headlines (+22 pts; 84% vs 61% district)
4. **Lesson artifacts cards** → **quote-wall**

## Demand-side fidelity

- **Shortlist signals honored**: student achievement/growth data in every experience headline; self-created materials as first-class cards; evidence of *students learning* (adoption by 6 schools, 300+ families engaged), not teacher credentials alone.
- **Bin signals avoided**: not a certificate-and-stock-template portfolio — credentials get one pane, outcomes get the rest; no identifiable student work or photos anywhere (FERPA gate enforced at the data layer, not just the markup).

## Extrapolation notes — what Hope generalizes vs what is Marisol's

1. **Generalizes**: the FERPA hard gate — anonymize all student work, block un-consented photos without re-asking. This is the pack's only non-negotiable and applies to every educator.
2. **Generalizes**: credentials-first-but-brief ordering, warm register, aggregate growth data in headlines.
3. **Marisol-specific**: veteran modulation — longitudinal growth data across cohorts. A first-year teacher's pack leads with student-teaching evidence and reflection quality instead (spec §3 modulation column).
4. **Marisol-specific**: the blocked gallery-grid. An educator who *does* hold signed consent files may enable it; the gate checks consent, not the app.
5. **Marisol-specific**: curriculum-adoption counts as the impact metric — generalizes only to teachers whose materials travel beyond their own classroom.
