# Nadia Okafor — writer pack sample persona

> **Fictional.** All handles carry the `-fictional` suffix (`muckrack.com/nadiaokafor-fictional`, `nadiaokafor-fictional.substack.com`), per the Jane Doe convention.

## Who she is

Freelance climate journalist on the energy beat. Ten years from city-council meetings to national features. Richmond, Virginia. Files clean and files on time — editors remember both.

## The arc

Medill, then the classic apprenticeship: three years at The Virginian-Pilot, 240+ bylines, mostly things nobody wanted to cover and a few things everybody later had to. E&E News made her an energy reporter — grid policy, FERC dockets, three features shortlisted for trade-press awards. A Knight Science Journalism fellowship year at MIT (2021–22) converted the beat knowledge into science fluency, and she went freelance in mid-2022: 41 features since, in The Atlantic, Grist, and MIT Technology Review. Since 2023 she also writes **Transmission**, a newsletter on the grid build-out — 5,200 subscribers, weekly, never missed.

Her portfolio is built the way an editor actually reads: the pitch first, then **two to three clips matched to the exact beat**, dated, outlet named, lede excerpt visible — because the demand side clicks one or two links at most and judges fit in the first paragraph. Recency is the signal, so everything carries a date and the top clip is never older than this quarter. Paywalled pieces link out; nothing is reproduced.

## Why the writer pack fits

- **"Selected clips" is the lead app** — headline + outlet + lede excerpt, date-sorted, outlet in the item meta — the pack's exact lead-app shape, and the inverse of a clip dump.
- **Shortlist signals she holds:** clips matched to the target beat (an editor hiring for energy sees energy, not her three best regardless of topic); error-free prose with a recognizable voice; relevance over prestige — a Grist grid piece outranks an older general-interest Atlantic piece when the req is energy.
- **Bin signals she avoids:** no 40-clip dump (3–8 cap, she ships 6); no off-beat samples; no making the editor hunt — one click from the hero to any clip; paywalled work linked, never pasted.
- **Recency date-stamps everywhere** — the cross-pack rule applies to her hardest, and the optional "Recent bylines" timeline makes the cadence visible.

## What she wants next

A staff writer seat on a climate desk — or a contributing-editor arrangement that keeps Transmission alive. She's honest that those pull in different directions.
