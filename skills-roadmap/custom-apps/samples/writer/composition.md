## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

The editor reads **the pitch first, clips second, clicks 1–2 links max**, and judges fit in the first paragraph. So:

1. **Hero** — name, "Climate journalist · energy & the grid" (the beat IS the identity line), one stat trio (41 features · 5,200 subscribers · 10 yrs on the beat)
2. **Selected clips** (default-open pane) — the top clip is on-beat and from this quarter; the lede excerpt does the first-paragraph audition without forcing a click
3. Recent bylines → Experience → Education; the newsletter card sits in Experience's sightline, not above the clips

Bin signals avoided by construction: no clip dump (6, curated to the energy beat), no off-beat samples (the food-section pieces stay in the graph, not on the page), no hunting (every clip is one click from the hero), zero tolerance for errors — the page itself is the writing sample.

## Extrapolation notes — what generalizes vs what is Nadia-specific

1. **Generalizes:** clips-as-lead-app with outlet + date + lede excerpt — every writer persona leads with the work itself, and the 3–8 cap holds regardless of how prolific the writer is.
2. **Generalizes:** beat-matched curation — Hope re-picks the visible clips per target outlet/req (relevance beats prestige); the full clip archive stays in the graph (both-ends rule).
3. **Nadia-specific:** the "Recent bylines" timeline earns its place because her cadence is genuinely steady. A writer with a 14-month gap skips it — the app would advertise the gap (R15-adjacent honesty; offer, don't assume).
4. **Nadia-specific:** the newsletter as a project card. It generalizes only to writers with owned platforms; for the content-marketing variant the spec adds business outcomes (traffic, conversion) to each clip instead.
5. **Generalizes:** paywall handling — link, never reproduce. Applies to every clip from any outlet, no persona exceptions.
