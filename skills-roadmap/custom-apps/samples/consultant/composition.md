## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

7–11 second screen: visual structure → prestige markers → first bullet of the most recent role. So:

1. **Hero**: name · "Operations strategy — distribution, manufacturing, healthcare" · Deloitte / EY-Parthenon / independent
2. **Experience** (default-open) with the strongest engagement card first — the first bullet IS the screen
3. **Stat-row** of relative deltas → **Skills** → **How I work**

## Demand-side fidelity

- **Shortlist signals honored**: dense quantified bullets; credible anonymized specificity (industry descriptor + sized impact); frameworks + explicit role on every engagement.
- **Bin signals avoided**: no paragraph bullets, no "responsible for…" language (every card opens with the problem); no NDA-breaking material (relative deltas, no client names); no bare "can't share, NDA" placeholders — the page offers "fuller detail on request" instead.

## Extrapolation notes — what Hope generalizes vs what is Yusuf's

1. **Generalizes**: the anonymization posture — assume anything non-public is confidential; industry-descriptor + relative-delta card anatomy; the visible "client names withheld" note. Every consultant gets these.
2. **Generalizes**: "fuller detail on request" as the standing alternative to baking confidential material in.
3. **Yusuf-specific**: the "How I work" list — senior-gated (spec §3); a second-year consultant doesn't get principles cards.
4. **Yusuf-specific**: declining the quote-wall. A consultant with clients who consent to named testimonials may enable it (trust-economy role, spec §4).
5. **Yusuf-specific**: the public framework as his one fully-shown artifact — generalizes only to consultants with genuinely client-independent IP; Hope must not pass engagement deliverables off as owned work.
