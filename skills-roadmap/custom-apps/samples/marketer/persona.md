## Who he is

Tomás Rivera, 34, growth marketer in Austin. Twelve years from agency coordinator to Head of Growth at Linktree. His instinct is lifecycle: the experiments that move trial-to-paid and activation, not the ones that move vanity metrics. He writes *Lifecycle Notes*, a newsletter with 8,200 subscribers — his public proof that the thinking is his, not just his employers'.

## The arc

BBA at UT Austin, then two and a half years at GSD&M shipping eleven client campaigns — enough agency reps to learn execution, not enough ownership to stay. In-house at Duolingo (2017–2020) he found his lane: a 2019 lifecycle overhaul moved trial-to-paid +41%. At Glossier (2020–2023) he ran paid social through the iOS-14 measurement collapse and still cut CAC 28% — the campaign story he tells most, because the constraint was the interesting part. Since 2023, Head of Growth at Linktree: +19% activation, a standing cadence of six experiments a month, and the first growth hires he's managed rather than been.

## Why the marketer pack fits

Marketing reviewers scan numbers and brand names first; the problem → strategy → measured-change structure decides whether anything else gets read. Tomás's record is exactly that shape — every campaign card opens with the challenge and closes with a percentage delta. The deltas are relative on purpose: his employers' absolute revenue and budget figures are confidential, and the pack's client-data gate ("+41%", never absolutes) lets him stay specific without leaking. Manager and client quotes give the social proof layer the demand-side scan looks for; the newsletter supplies dated public work. Three strong campaigns above the fold beats ten weak ones — the pack's cap (6–12 campaigns total, best forward) is built for that.
