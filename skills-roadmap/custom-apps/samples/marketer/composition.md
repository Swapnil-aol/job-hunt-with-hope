## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

Reviewers scan numbers and brand names first; problem–solution–results structure decides whether anything else gets read. So:

1. **Hero**: name · "Growth marketer — lifecycle & paid" · Duolingo / Glossier / Linktree named
2. **Results stat-row** directly under the hero — the numbers land before the first scroll
3. **Experience** (default-open): best three campaigns first, Challenge→Strategy→Execution→Results
4. **Quote-wall** → **Skills** → depth sections (both-ends rule: process detail inside expanded cards)

## Demand-side fidelity

- **Shortlist signals honored**: problem → strategy → measured change on every card; manager/client quotes as social proof; 7 campaigns within the 6–12 cap, strongest first.
- **Bin signals avoided**: no deliverable gallery with zero numbers (gallery-grid declined); no mock work — every campaign is attributed to a real employer engagement; three strong above the fold, not ten weak.

## Extrapolation notes — what Hope generalizes vs what is Tomás's

1. **Generalizes**: Challenge→Strategy→Execution→Results card anatomy, stat-row under the hero, relative-delta gate on confidential figures — every marketer gets these.
2. **Generalizes**: quote-wall eligibility — but Hope harvests quotes only from material the user provides; it never invents attribution.
3. **Tomás-specific**: declining the campaign-creative gallery. A brand or content marketer with owned creative flips that switch on.
4. **Tomás-specific**: the newsletter as dated public proof — freelance-cut marketers would instead surface niche + named testimonials + explicit services (spec §3 modulation column).
5. **Tomás-specific**: senior modulation — strategy + budget scale framing. A junior marketer's cards lead with channel execution proof instead.
