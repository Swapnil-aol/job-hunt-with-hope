## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

Decision in the first 10 seconds of the reel, frequently on mute. So:

1. **Hero**: name · "Editor & motion — doc + brand" · one line, minimal copy
2. **Reel** (default-open): 75 seconds, best ten seconds first, mute-proof; thumbnail + worded link
3. **Selected work grid** (8 pieces, range across doc/brand/motion) → **Credits cards** → **Experience**

## Demand-side fidelity

- **Shortlist signals honored**: best 10 seconds first; 75s total (inside the 60–90s window); mute-proof cut; 8 curated pieces; client-approved work foregrounded (revision-cycle survival); deliberate range across three formats.
- **Bin signals avoided**: no long reel burying the best shot; no choice-paralysis book (8, not 30); no all-spec work (29 client deliveries anchor the grid); no one-style lock — and the rights gate holds: embargoed campaign absent, no unlicensed-music claims, client footage only with permission.

## Extrapolation notes — what Hope generalizes vs what is Lena's

1. **Generalizes**: reel-first emphasis, baked-thumbnail video treatment (self-contained rule), mandatory per-clip contribution labels — every creative-media persona gets these; collaborative work claimed whole is the pack's cardinal sin.
2. **Generalizes**: the rights gate — client permissions, music licensing, embargoes — enforced at the data layer (absent, not hidden).
3. **Lena-specific**: the doc/brand split as the range story. A motion-only designer shows range via format and pace instead; Hope reads range from the actual body of work, never demands a second genre.
4. **Lena-specific**: senior modulation — client work + range = directability. A junior's grid leads with craft moments (spec §3 modulation column).
5. **Lena-specific**: "9 repeat clients" as the trust metric — generalizes only where the engagement history supports it; Hope must not infer repeat business from a client list.
