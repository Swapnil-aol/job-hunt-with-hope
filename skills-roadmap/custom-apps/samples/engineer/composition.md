## Above-the-fold order (per the demand-side scan)

The scan is ~90 seconds on GitHub, and only if linked: profile → pinned repos → one README. The page mirrors that:

1. **Hero** — name, "Staff platform engineer", the GitHub link, three terse stats (2.1k stars · 11 Prometheus PRs · deploys 37min → 6min)
2. **Projects** (default-open pane) — 3 cards, each headline carrying the outcome number; problem/approach/results inside the expanded card (both-ends rule: demand wins the fold, supply wins the depth)
3. Experience → Skills → Education, then the informal apps at the bottom

Bin signals avoided by construction: no streak talk, no fork walls, no quote-wall (pack-banned), no repo-count padding — 3 projects, inside the 3–5 cap.

## Extrapolation notes — what generalizes vs what is Marcus-specific

1. **Generalizes:** the employer-IP gate's *shape* — when paid work is private, OSS and side projects carry the proof load. Any engineer in fintech/healthcare/defense gets this composition; for gov/defense the spec goes further (clearance up front, empty GitHub unpenalized).
2. **Generalizes:** terse, metric-headline cards and worded repo anchors — that's the pack's register, not Marcus's taste.
3. **Marcus-specific:** the "train" traveler, the throughput/latency framing of his arc, and the particular repo trio. Another engineer's story might be breadth (consultancy) or depth in one system.
4. **Marcus-specific:** "/uses" shipping at all — it only renders because his list is genuinely current (R15). Most users won't have one; the pack offers, never assumes.
5. **Generalizes:** the timeline carries roles + dated projects; dateless side quests live only in the "Side quests" app (one-home rule) — same curation Jane's sample documents.
