# Diego Fuentes — data pack sample persona

> **Fictional.** All handles carry the `-fictional` suffix (`github.com/diegofuentes-fictional`, `kaggle.com/diegofuentes-fictional`), per the Jane Doe convention.

## Who he is

Senior data scientist at Oscar Health. Nine years from grocery-chain analyst to healthcare ML. Austin. Believes the modeling is the easy 20% and says so in public.

## The arc

Statistics at UT Austin, then H-E-B — inventory shrink down 14% across two years of unglamorous SKU-level analysis, where he learned that 80% of the job is the dirty data nobody mentions in the course catalog. Instacart put him on demand forecasting: −23% WAPE against the incumbent model, mostly won in feature pipelines, not architecture. Oscar Health since 2022: a readmission-risk model lifted from AUC 0.81 to 0.86, now in front of care teams.

None of his employer data can ever be shown — groceries, then PHI. So his public work is **rebuilds of the techniques on public data**, exactly the pack's proprietary-data gate: NYC 311 response times (a 4.1-day borough gap, found after cleaning 1.2M messy rows), ERCOT grid-demand forecasting (MAPE 4.2% vs a 6.8% seasonal baseline), and a claims-forecasting rebuild on CMS public data (−18% WAPE vs seasonal naive). Every README answers the same four questions — what problem, what data, why this approach, results *and limitations* — and every project starts at the raw, dirty source, not at the modeling step. His PyData Austin talk is titled "Eight forecasting failures" on purpose: what-didn't-work is the senior signal.

## Why the data pack fits

- **Projects lead, each card opening on the question answered and the metric moved**, one chart image per card — the pack's exact card grammar.
- **Shortlist signals he holds:** READMEs with problem/data/approach/results+limitations; end-to-end pipelines including the wrangling; code behind every claim; a public "what didn't work" record.
- **Bin signals he avoids:** zero Titanic/MNIST/iris; not Kaggle-only (the handle exists, the portfolio doesn't lean on it); no project starts at modeling; three different techniques across three projects — no one-hammer.
- **Honest scope:** each rebuild states plainly that it's a public-data reconstruction of a private technique, not the production system.

## What he wants next

A staff DS role where the forecasting is the business, not a dashboard garnish — energy, logistics, or healthcare ops.
