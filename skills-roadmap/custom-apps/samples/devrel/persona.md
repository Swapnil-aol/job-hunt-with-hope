## The arc

CS at the University of Washington, then four years shipping public APIs as a backend engineer at Twilio. The pivot happened sideways: his OSS CLI (`httpcheck`, 2.1k stars) and the docs PRs he kept filing got noticed, and in 2019 he moved into developer advocacy at the same company — 38 talks across 12 countries over three years, plus 48 merged docs PRs, the metric he cites when someone implies devrel is just stage time. At Supabase since 2022, he's narrowed deliberately: Postgres for application developers, nothing else. Specialization beats breadth, and his calendar proves it.

## Why the devrel pack fits

DevRel has no dedicated demand-side study — reviewers inherit the engineer scan (GitHub, ~90 seconds, only if linked) and the writer recency norms. That means the public footprint *is* the résumé: the pack leads with a "Talks & podcasts" list, 1–3 signature items pinned first, each with title + event + year + a worded video link. Everything dated — the cross-pack recency rule applies hardest here, and Kenji's data carries `last_updated`-style date stamps on the blog and the /uses page (R15: a stale /uses gets omitted, with a refresh offer, rather than shipped stale). Videos render as baked thumbnails linking out, never downloaded — the self-contained rule holds. GitHub renders prominently in the contact line: its absence would be a negative signal in this field.
